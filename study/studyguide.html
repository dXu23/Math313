Exercise 1.2.1
  1. Prove that \(\sqrt{3}\) is irrational. Does a similar argument work to show that \(\sqrt{6}\) is irrational?

    We assume by contradiction that \(\sqrt{3}\) is rational. Then $$\begin{eqnarray} \sqrt{3} & = & \frac{p}{q} \\ 3 & = & \frac{p^{2}}{q^{2}} \\ 3q^{2} & = & p^{2} \\ 3q^{2} & = & (3k)^{2} \end{eqnarray}$$
